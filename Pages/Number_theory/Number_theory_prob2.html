Mathematically Hard

Link :https://lightoj.com/problem/mathematically-hard

Problem summary:

          We are given two integers a and b. We will have to find scores of all the numbers ranging from a to b. Here,
          score(x)=n2 (n = number of integers from 1 to n that are comprime with x)
          i.e. no. of integers such that gcd(x,i)=1 (1<=i<=n)

Solution:

          We know that, Euler's totient function gives # of coprimes from 1 to n with
            ɸ(n) = n * (1-1/p1) * (1-1/p2) ...(1-1/pk , where p1,p2,p3...pk are prime factors of n.
            we will use sieve to find phi(n) . In sieve while a number x is divisible by a prime factor p . we will divide x by p and multiply it by x-1
            i.e. phi[x]/=x and p[x]*=(x-1)
            we will also have to use prefix sum techinique res = Pref_sum[b]-Pref_sum[a-1]. Otherwise we will get TLE
